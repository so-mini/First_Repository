Chewy Chocolate Chip Cookies

Prep Time Cooking Time Total Time
1 hr 15 mins 1 hr 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 155g flour.
  • 40g ground sugar(165 g), packed
  • 80g brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 115g unsalted butter (melted)
  • 1 tspn vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 dark chocolate bar
  • 110g chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugars, salt, and butter until a paste forms.
  2. Whisk in the egg and vanilla.
  3. Sift in the flour and baking soda, then fold into the mixture.
  4. Fold in the chocolate, leaving some behind for when baking, then chill the dough for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  6. Scoop the dough onto a parchment paper-lined baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes.
  8. Cool completely before serving.

Peanut Butter Cookies

Prep Time Cooking Time Total Time
15 mins 30 mins 45 mins

Ingredients

  • 110g unsalted butter
  • 120g peanut butter
  • 120g brown sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter(115 g), melted
  • 1 egg
  • 200g all-purpose flour
  • 3g baking powder

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the butter (room temperature) and peanut butter until smooth.
  2. Whisk in the brown sugar and mix well.
  3. Put in the egg and incorporate evenly.
  4. Add in the all-purpose flour and fold carefully until a smooth lump forms.
  5. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes.
  7. Cool completely before serving.

Meringue Cookies

Prep Time Cooking Time Total Time
20 mins 2 hrs 2 hrs 20 mins

Ingredients

  • 4 large egg whites room temperature
  • ½ teaspoon cream of tartar
  • ⅛ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar (200g)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 225F (105C) and line a large cookie sheet (or two regular-sized cookie sheets -- make sure they will fit in your oven together) with parchment paper. Set aside.
  2. Combine egg whites, cream of tartar, and salt in a large, completely clean, completely grease-free bow
  3. Using an electric mixer or a stand mixer (with either the whisk or paddle attachment), stir on low speed until mixture becomes foamy.
  4. With mixer on high, gradually add sugar, about 1 Tablespoon at a time, stirring after each addition until sugar is dissolved (about 15-20 seconds between each addition).
  5. Beat until mixture is thick, shiny, and has increased in volume. Mixture should have stiff peaks and sugar should be completely dissolved (you can test this by rubbing a small bit of the mixture between your fingers, if it feels gritty, the sugar isn't dissolved)
  6. Stir in vanilla extract and any other extract you may like to use. If using food coloring, add the food coloring at this stage, too.
  7. Fit a large disposable piping bag with a large tip (I used Ateco 846, but any large star tip will work or you can just snip the edge of the piping bag for a “kiss” formation) and transfer meringue to prepared piping bag and pipe onto prepared cookie sheet. The meringue cookies can be pretty close to each other as they won’t spread, and you will want to bake all of the cookies at the same time, so make sure you make enough space.
  8. Bake on 225F (105C) for 1 hour. Turn off the oven once the baking time has passed, and do not open the oven. Leave the oven door closed and allow cookies to cool completely in the oven (1-2 hours) before removing.
